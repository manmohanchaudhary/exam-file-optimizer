# **Bihar Assistant Professor Recruitment 2026: Notification, Eligibility, Exam Pattern & Vacancy Details**

The Bihar State University Service Commission (BSUSC) is preparing to launch one of the largest academic recruitment drives in the state's history. Under the ambitious "Saat Nischay-3 (2025-30)" development program and its "Unnat Shiksha Ujjwal Bhavishya" (Advanced Education, Bright Future) initiative, the Government of Bihar has officially approved the creation of 9,152 posts. This historic recruitment campaign aims to operationalize 208 newly established degree colleges across every block in the state, significantly expanding higher education access to rural areas.  
For academic job seekers across India, this represents a premier opportunity to secure a regular or contractual faculty position in Bihar's state universities. Below is a comprehensive guide to the Bihar Assistant Professor 2026 recruitment, containing eligibility criteria, selection frameworks, syllabus details, exam patterns, and application instructions.

## **Key Highlights of the New 2026 Recruitment Statutes**

On June 22, 2026, the Governor-cum-Chancellor of Bihar, Syed Ata Hasnain, gave official assent to the new "Statutes for Appointment of Assistant Professors, 2026". Implemented across all state universities on July 1, 2026, this framework brings critical structural updates compared to the previous 2020 guidelines:

* **Standardized Written Exam:** Selection is no longer based solely on academic record scores (API index). It now requires a robust descriptive written examination of 175 marks, ensuring evaluation objectivity.  
* **Teaching Demonstration:** The interview phase (25 marks) is highly structured and includes a mandatorily video-recorded teaching skills demonstration carrying 13 marks.  
* **Lowered Minimum Age:** The minimum age limit has been lowered to 21 years (calculated as of July 1st of the advertisement year) from the previously proposed 23 years.  
* **Adjusted Maximum Age:** The final approved statutes cap the maximum age limit at 43 years as of August 1st of the advertisement year, providing relief over the initially drafted 40-year limit.  
* **Emergent Contractual Hiring:** A new statutory chapter facilitates centralized contractual appointments of Assistant Professors to immediately support newly established colleges prior to regular appointments.

## **Academic Eligibility and Exemption Framework**

Candidates must fulfill rigorous academic and testing standards to qualify for recruitment.

### **1. Educational Qualifications & Grade Relaxations**

* **Master’s Degree:** Applicants must possess a Master's degree in the concerned, relevant, or allied subject with at least 55% marks (or an equivalent grade) from a UGC-recognized university.  
* **Reserved Category Relaxation:** Candidates with a valid Bihar domicile belonging to SC, ST, EBC (Non-creamy layer), or BC (Non-creamy layer) receive a 5% relaxation, bringing their minimum qualification marks down to 50%.  
* **PwD Category Relaxation:** Eligible Persons with Disabilities (PwD) across India also receive a 5% marks relaxation. This relaxation is calculated strictly based on qualifying marks without incorporating grace marks.

### **2. National & State-Level Test Eligibility**

Candidates must have qualified in the National Eligibility Test (NET) conducted by the UGC or CSIR, or a State-Level Eligibility Test (SLET/SET). Note that SLET/SET qualifications are accepted only if they were conducted by or on behalf of the Government of Bihar.

### **3. Comprehensive Ph.D. Exemption Criteria**

Candidates holding a Ph.D. degree awarded in accordance with the UGC Regulations of 2009 or 2016 are exempted from qualifying NET/SLET/SET. For candidates registered prior to July 11, 2009, the eligibility test waiver is valid only if they present a Registrar-certified compliance document confirming:

1. The Ph.D. was awarded in regular mode.  
2. The thesis was evaluated by at least two external examiners.  
3. An open Ph.D. viva voce was successfully conducted.  
4. The candidate published at least two research papers from their doctoral work, with at least one in a peer-reviewed or refereed journal.  
5. The candidate presented at least two papers based on their Ph.D. work in UGC/ICSSR/CSIR-recognized conferences or seminars.

### **Stage 1: Descriptive Written Examination (175 Marks)**

All eligible applicants must appear for a three-hour subjective exam based strictly on the UGC NET syllabus of the respective subject. The exam contains a total of 60 descriptive questions distributed as follows:

| Question Format | Number of Questions | Marks per Question | Total Marks | Targeted Skills Assessed |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Very Short Answer** | 20 | 1 | 20 Marks | Precision recall, definitions, and academic terminology. |
| **Short Answer** | 15 | 2 | 30 Marks | Conceptual differentiation and analytical summaries. |
| **Reasoning Analytical** | 15 | 3 | 45 Marks | Practical application of logical frameworks. |
| **Long Answer** | 10 | 8 | 80 Marks | In-depth thematic synthesis and domain expertise. |
| **Total Blueprint** | **60 Questions** | **—** | **175 Marks** | Comprehensive academic capacity and writing expertise. |

To be considered for the shortlisting stage, candidates must achieve the following category-wise minimum qualifying marks in the written test:

* **General (Unreserved):** 40.0% (70 Marks)  
* **Backward Class (BC):** 36.5% (63.87 Marks)  
* **Extremely Backward Class (EBC):** 34.0% (59.50 Marks)  
* **SC / ST / Women (All Categories):** 32.0% (56 Marks)

### **Stage 2: Shortlisting and Selection Board Interview (25 Marks)**

From the qualifying pool, candidates are shortlisted in a strict 1:3 ratio per advertised vacancy in each subject category. Shortlisted candidates face an interview board comprising three subject experts of Professor rank. Under the 2026 statutes, the board must include at least one expert representing the SC, ST, BC, EBC, Women, or PwD categories.

* **Teaching Skill Demonstration (13 Marks):** Evaluates presentation structure, blackboard utilization, and communication skills. The demonstration is mandatorily video-recorded to ensure complete transparency.  
* **Board Interaction (12 Marks):** Evaluates subject depth, research background, and general pedagogical suitability.

## **Centralized Contractual Assistant Professor Recruitment**

To ensure that teaching in newly created colleges commences immediately, the BSUSC will hire over 2,500 contractual Assistant Professors. This centralized, merit-based contractual appointment runs on a distinct non-exam tracking system:

* **Academic Performance Index (88 Marks):** Aggregates scores across Graduation, Post-Graduation, M.Phil/Ph.D., research publications, teaching experience, and awards.  
* **Interview Process (12 Marks):** Divided into a 6-mark teaching skills presentation (video-recorded) and a 6-mark board interaction.  
* **Compensation:** Selected contractual teachers receive a consolidated basic pay of ₹57,700 per month plus applicable Dearness Allowance (DA).

## **Step-by-Step Online Application Protocol**

Candidates must submit their applications via the official BSUSC online system. Duplicate profiles or submissions using different Candidate IDs will lead to automatic rejection.

1. **Access Portal:** Navigate to bsusc.bihar.gov.in and access the active recruitment application page.  
2. **OTR Registration:** Create a profile by inputting your name, mobile number, and email ID to receive your unique login credentials.  
3. **Application Form:** Log in and complete your educational, personal, and communication profiles.  
4. **Document Upload:** Upload scanned copies of the required credentials in JPG/PDF formats.  
5. **Fee Payment:** Submit the processing fee via online payment modes (Credit Card, Debit Card, Net Banking, or UPI).

6. **Final Preview & Print:** Review all sections, finalize the application, and print the confirmation copy for document verification.

## **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)**

### **What is the maximum age limit for applying under the 2026 Statute?**

The maximum age limit is capped at 43 years as of August 1st of the advertisement year. Relaxations for SC, ST, EBC, BC, Women, and PwD categories are provided as per the prevailing guidelines of the Government of Bihar and the Government of India.

### **Is there a descriptive test or an MCQ exam for selection?**

The written test is entirely subjective and descriptive, with a duration of 3 hours. It contains 60 descriptive questions carrying a total of 175 marks. The syllabus matches that of the UGC NET in the respective discipline.

### **Are teaching demonstrations recorded during the interview?**

Yes. To maintain complete transparency and eliminate bias, the 2026 Statutes mandate that the 13-mark teaching skills demonstration must be video-recorded in real-time.

### **What happens if a candidate submits a fake certificate?**

Under the enacted 2026 Statutes, any candidate found submitting a forged document faces immediate cancellation of their candidature, along with a strict five-year ban from all state public sector recruitments and potential criminal prosecution.

### **Can PG students in their final year apply for the post?**

Generally, applicants must possess their completed Master's degree certificate and consolidated marksheets at the time of online application verification, unless explicitly relaxed in the official subject notification. Ensure you check your subject-wise notification details once released on the BSUSC website.
