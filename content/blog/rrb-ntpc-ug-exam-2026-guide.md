## **How to Check the RRB NTPC City Intimation Slip & Download Admit Card**

To combat cheating syndicates, the RRB follows a strict timeline for releasing venue information.

* **T-10 Protocol (City Intimation Slip):** Exactly 10 days prior to your specific exam date, the RRB will release a City Intimation Slip. This document tells you your assigned exam city and shift so you can book travel tickets. However, it will *not* contain the specific college or test center.
* **T-4 Protocol (E-Call Letter/Admit Card):** The official Admit Card—which includes your exact exam venue, address, and barcode—is released exactly 4 days before your exam date.

### **Steps to Download:**

1. Visit your regional RRB website.  
2. Look for the "CEN 07/2025 (NTPC-Undergraduate): CBT-1 City-Intimation / E-Call Letter" link.  
3. Enter your Registration Number and Date of Birth (as the password).  
4. Download and take a clear physical printout of the E-Call Letter to carry to the center.

## **Crucial Exam Day Guidelines: Aadhaar Biometric Verification**

Gone are the days of simple physical ID checks. The RRB now mandates **Aadhaar-linked biometric authentication** at the venue gates.  
**Important Warning:** If your Aadhaar biometric data is "locked" in the UIDAI system, the venue scanners will reject you, and you will be denied entry.

### **How to Unlock Your Aadhaar for the Exam:**

1. Obtain your 16-digit Virtual ID (VID) by sending an SMS to 1947 with the text RVID [space].  
2. Visit the official UIDAI portal at https://resident.uidai.gov.in/aadhaar-lockunlock.  
3. Select the "Unlock" radio button, enter your 16-digit VID, and input the security captcha. 
4. Authenticate via OTP to successfully unlock your biometrics for exam day.

## **Free Travel Pass for SC/ST Candidates**

To bridge the socio-economic divide, the RRB provides a Free Sleeper Class Railway Pass for eligible SC and ST candidates. This pass is integrated directly into the portal alongside the City Intimation Slip. Candidates must present this downloaded authority along with their *original* caste certificate to the Traveling Ticket Examiner (TTE) during their train journey.
