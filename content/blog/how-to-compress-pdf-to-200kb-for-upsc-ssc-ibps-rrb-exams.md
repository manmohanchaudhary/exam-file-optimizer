Struggling with the strict document upload limits for government exams? Whether you are applying for **UPSC Civil Services**, **SSC CGL**, **IBPS PO**, or **Railway (RRB)** exams, you will face a common challenge: your Aadhaar Card, Caste Certificate, or Marksheets must be in **PDF format** and strictly under **200KB or 500KB**.

Uploading a file that is even 1KB over the limit results in a "File Size Exceeded" error. In this guide, we’ll show you how to shrink your PDF while keeping it 100% legible for official verification.

## Document Specifications for Major Exams (2026)

Different exam boards have different "sweet spots" for file sizes. Here is a quick reference table to help you target the right size:

| Exam Body | Document Type | Typical PDF Limit | Required Format |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| UPSC (IAS, NDA, CDS) | Certificates/ID | 20KB – 300KB | PDF Only |
| SSC (CGL, CHSL, MTS) | Documents | Usually < 200KB | PDF / JPG |
| IBPS / SBI (Bank Exams) | Marksheets/Handwritten | < 500KB | PDF |
| RRB (Railways) | Category Certificates | 50KB – 300KB | PDF |
| State PSCs (BPSC, UPPSC) | All Documents | 100KB – 500KB | PDF |

## Common Errors to Avoid

* **Invalid File Names:** Avoid names like `final_document_v2(1).pdf`. Use simple names like `Aadhaar_Card.pdf` or `Caste_Certificate.pdf`.
* **Scanning via WhatsApp:** Never use a photo sent over WhatsApp; the compression is too high and the quality is too low for official use.
* **Too Small:** Files under **20KB** are often rejected as "low resolution." Aim for the **150KB–190KB** range for a 200KB limit to ensure maximum quality.
