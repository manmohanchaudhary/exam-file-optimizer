The Air Force Common Admission Test (AFCAT) is one of the most prestigious defense exams in India. It also has one of the most unique image upload requirements: the mandatory "Slate Photo." Failing to follow the strict guidelines for this specific photograph is a surefire way to get your application rejected.

## The 'Black Slate' Requirement Explained

Unlike standard passport photos, AFCAT requires male and female candidates to hold a physical black slate in front of their chest. 

* **Positioning:** Hold the slate clearly in front of your chest.
* **What to Write:** You must write your **Full Name** and the **Date of Photograph**.
* **How to Write:** The text must be written clearly using **White Chalk**. Do not use digital text or editing software to add the text later.

## AFCAT Technical Specifications Table

Beyond the slate, the digital files you upload must meet precise technical criteria:

| Document | Width x Height | File Size (KB) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Passport Photo | 200 x 230 px | 10 KB to 50 KB |
| Signature | 140 x 60 px | 10 KB to 50 KB |
| Thumb Impression | 200 x 230 px | 10 KB to 50 KB |


## Step-by-Step: Capturing the Perfect AFCAT Photo

1. **Get a Real Slate:** Purchase a small black slate and white chalk.
2. **Write Clearly:** Write your name and the current date in capital letters. Ensure it is legible.
3. **Lighting & Background:** Stand against a **light-colored background** (preferably white). Ensure the lighting is bright enough so your face and the chalk writing are clearly visible without shadows.
4. **Take the Photo:** Have someone take the photo from the chest up, ensuring the slate and your face are in sharp focus.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

* **Blurry Text on the Slate:** If the chalk writing is out of focus or too small to read, your photo will be rejected.
* **Using a Digital "Slate":** Do not take a normal photo and digitally edit a black box with text over your chest. The IAF strictly checks for authentic slate photos.
* **Wrong File Naming:** AFCAT sometimes requires specific file naming conventions (e.g., naming your file `Passport_Photo.jpg`). Always check the official notification for naming rules before uploading.

## FAQs

**Q: Can I use a slate photo from my previous AFCAT attempt?**
A: No. The date written on the slate must be recent, typically taken after the notification release date. Using an old photo will lead to rejection.

**Q: Is a left-thumb impression mandatory for both males and females?**
A: Yes, for AFCAT, the left-thumb impression is generally required for both male and female candidates. Ensure the impression is clear and not smudged.
