The National Testing Agency (NTA) has officially reached a critical milestone in the NEET UG 2026 cycle with the release of the **Advance Intimation of Examination City** on **April 12, 2026**. This development provides over **2.6 million registered candidates**—a record-breaking figure for the medical entrance exam—with the necessary details to finalize their logistical arrangements for the test scheduled on **May 3, 2026**.

## **Current Development: Advanced City Intimation**

As of **April 13, 2026**, the download link for the city intimation slip is live on the official portal. This document serves as a preliminary logistical notice and is distinct from the formal admit card.

### **Key Purpose and Immediate Utility**

* **Travel Planning:** Informs candidates of their allotted city and state to allow for early booking of transport and accommodation.  
* **Verification:** Allows candidates to confirm that their application has been successfully processed and their data is correctly reflected in the NTA system.  
* **Logistical synchronous:** Ensures that the unprecedented volume of students can reach **552 domestic cities** and **14 international centers** without last-minute travel congestion.

## **Current Security Protocols and Allotment Logic**

A major development this year is the implementation of stricter identity-verified center allocation. Unlike the flexible selection models of previous years, 2026 center allotment was governed by the following "Current Rules":

1. **Aadhaar-Linked eKYC:** Address verification was strictly tied to the candidate's Aadhaar-registered data during the registration window.  
2. **Live Photo Capture:** NTA utilized live photograph capture to prevent impersonation and mismatch issues seen in earlier cycles.  
3. **Proximity Allotment:** Candidates were restricted to three city choices strictly within the state of their present or permanent address to ensure local geographic clusters.

### **Discrepancy Management**

If any discrepancy is found in the current city slip (e.g., name, photograph, or signature), candidates are instructed to contact the NTA Help Desk immediately:
