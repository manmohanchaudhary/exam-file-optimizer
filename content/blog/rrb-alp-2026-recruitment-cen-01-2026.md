If you're interested in joining Indian Railways as an Assistant Loco Pilot, here's everything you need to know about this recruitment.

## The Opportunity

This is a massive recruitment push. The Railway Board has approved 11,127 Assistant Loco Pilot posts for recruitment in 2026, representing one of the biggest technical recruitment cycles in recent memory. These are Level-2 positions, and they're spread across various Railway zones throughout the country.

## Who Can Apply?

Before you rush to fill out an application, make sure you meet the eligibility criteria. 

* **Age Limit:** Applicants for the Assistant Loco Pilot position should be between **18 and 30 years old**. 
* **Educational Qualification:** Candidates must have **10th pass with ITI** in relevant trade or **Diploma in Engineering**. 

:::note
This is a pretty straightforward requirement—if you've completed your 10th and have the relevant technical qualification, you're on the right track.
:::

## The Exam Structure

The recruitment process isn't just a single exam. The RRB ALP Exam Pattern 2026 consists of three stages which are CBT 1, CBT 2 and Computer-Based Aptitude Test (CBAT). Let's break this down so you understand what you're getting into.

:::step{number="1" title="CBT 1 (Computer Based Test 1)"}
The first stage includes 75 questions for 75 marks covering Mathematics, Mental Ability, General Awareness and other subjects. The duration is 60 minutes (1 hour) with a Negative Marking of ⅓ mark for each incorrect answer. That means every wrong answer costs you, so accuracy matters.
:::

:::step{number="2" title="CBT 2 (Computer Based Test 2)"}
CBT 2 is tougher and is conducted for 175 marks, making it a significantly larger exam. This stage goes deeper into technical knowledge and is where candidates really need to demonstrate their competency.
:::

:::step{number="3" title="CBAT (Computer-Based Aptitude Test)"}
Candidates who qualify in the CBT 1 and 2 are eligible to attend the CBAT. You need minimum qualifying marks of 42 marks in each of the test batteries, irrespective of the category. This evaluates your suitability for the role.
:::

## The Salary Package

Let's talk money. The initial salary of selected candidates for Assistant Loco Pilot posts will be approximately **Rs. 19,900**. But here's the good part—that's just the starting point. The Railway Loco Pilot's monthly salary ranges between ₹19,900 and ₹35,000, inclusive of regular increments and additional benefits as per government norms.

If you want to understand your in-hand salary better, after the 7th Pay Commission, the RRB Assistant Loco Pilot (ALP) Salary includes:
* **Basic Pay:** ~₹19,900
* **Dearness Allowance (DA):** ~₹10,752
* **Running Allowance:** ~₹6,050
* **Gross Pay:** ~₹26,752

After deductions like NPS contributions, your in-hand salary will be slightly lower, but still a solid income for this level of position.

Here's the reality: this exam is competitive. With over 11,000 positions and potentially millions of applications, you need to be strategic in your preparation.

The CBAT is about assessing your aptitude, so that's less about memorization and more about your logical thinking and problem-solving abilities.
