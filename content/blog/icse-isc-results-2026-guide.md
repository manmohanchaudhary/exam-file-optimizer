## **How to Check ICSE and ISC Results 2026 Online**

Once the results are formally approved, the CISCE activates links on its two primary portals. To access your scorecard, you will need your Unique ID (UID) and Index Number, which are printed on your 2026 admit card.

### **Step-by-Step Guide for Official Websites**

1. Visit the official result portal: **results.cisce.org** or **cisce.org**.
2. From the "Course" dropdown menu, select either **ICSE** (Class 10) or **ISC** (Class 12).
3. Enter your seven-digit **Unique ID**.
4. Enter your seven-digit **Index Number**.
5. Type in the **CAPTCHA** code exactly as shown on the screen.
6. Click on the "Show Result" or "Submit" button.

## **Alternative Methods to Check Your Scores**

During the peak hours of result declaration, official websites often experience high traffic. The Council provides several alternative platforms to ensure every student can access their results without delay.

### **1. Check Results via SMS**

If you have limited internet access, you can receive your results directly on your mobile phone:

* **For ICSE (Class 10):** Type ICSE <7-Digit Unique ID> and send it to **09248082883**.
* **For ISC (Class 12):** Type ISC <7-Digit Unique ID> and send it to **09248082883**.

### **2. DigiLocker and UMANG App**

The CISCE issues digitally authenticated marksheets with QR codes, which are legally valid for college admissions.

* **DigiLocker:** Log in using your Aadhaar-linked mobile number, search for "Council for the Indian School Certificate Examinations," and enter your UID and Index Number to "pull" your digital marksheet.
* **UMANG App:** Open the app, search for "CISCE," select your respective class result link, and enter your credentials to download the PDF scorecard.

## **The 2026 Geopolitical Disruption: UAE Exam Cancellation**

A unique aspect of the 2026 examination cycle was the total cancellation of all board exams at centers in the United Arab Emirates (UAE). Due to escalating security tensions in the Middle East involving regional powers, the Council prioritized student safety over physical testing.

Students in the UAE will be evaluated using an **alternative assessment system**, likely based on internal marks and previous academic performance. The Council has assured these students that those dissatisfied with their "imputed marks" will have the opportunity to appear for physical improvement exams once regional conditions stabilize.

## **What After Results? Rechecking and Re-evaluation**

If you believe your marks do not accurately reflect your performance, the CISCE offers a formal redressal mechanism. It is important to act quickly, as the application windows are very short.

### **Rechecking Process (Scrutiny)**

Rechecking is an administrative review to ensure all answers were marked and that no totaling errors occurred.

* **Fee:** ₹1,000 per subject.
* **Window:** The portal typically opens on result day and stays active for only **four to seven days**.
* **Result:** Revised marks are uploaded directly to DigiLocker.

### **Re-evaluation of Answer Scripts**

Re-evaluation is a deeper dive into the subjective marking and is only available to students who have already completed the rechecking process. The window for re-evaluation usually opens for just three days after the recheck results are announced.

## **Improvement Examinations: A Second Chance**

For the 2026 session, the older "Compartment" model has been fully integrated into the **Improvement Examination** framework. This allows students to enhance their grades in the same academic year.

| Feature | ICSE (Class 10) | ISC (Class 12) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Max Subjects** | Up to 3 Subjects | Up to 2 Subjects |
| **Tentative Dates** | July 1 – July 14, 2026 | July 1 – July 17, 2026 |
| **Application Period** | Late May / Early June | May 30 – June 5, 2026 |
| **Eligibility** | Must have appeared in Main 2026 Exam | Must have appeared in Main 2026 Exam |

Note: The higher of the two marks (Main vs. Improvement) will be considered for the final certificate.

## **Passing Criteria and Standardized Fees for 2026**

To qualify for a pass certificate in 2026, students must meet specific percentage thresholds and internal requirements:

* **ICSE Passing Marks:** 33% in five subjects (including English and SUPW).
* **ISC Passing Marks:** 35% in four subjects (including English and SUPW).

### **New Standardized Fee Structure**

Starting in 2026, the Council standardized local examination fees to ensure uniformity across all schools.

| Category | ICSE (Class 10) | ISC (Class 12) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Indian Candidates** | ₹4,350 | ₹5,450 |
| **Overseas Candidates** | N/A (Due to Cancellation) | ₹10,000 (Base) |
| **Migration Certificate** | ₹250 | ₹250 |

This revised fee now covers all local expenses, meaning schools are no longer permitted to collect additional "center charges" or "stationery fees" from parents.
