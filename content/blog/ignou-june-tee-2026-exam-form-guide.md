#### **What is the IGNOU June TEE 2026 exam form?**
The June Term-End Examination (TEE) is IGNOU’s primary semester/term exam held once a year around June–July for all ODL and Online programmes. To sit in these exams, every student must fill a separate online exam form for that specific session, even if they are already registered or re-registered in the programme. Without submitting this form and paying the exam fee, no hall ticket is generated and you cannot appear in the June 2026 TEE.

---

#### **Key Dates for June TEE 2026 Exam Form**
The following timeline is based on official notices and consistent summaries from education portals:

| Event | Date / Window |
| --- | --- |
| Exam form start | From 10–11 March 2026 (effectively from 10 March onwards) |
| Last date (no late fee) | 10 April 2026 |
| With late fee window | 11 April 2026 to 24 April 2026 |
| Late fee amount | ₹1,100 (flat) |
| Exam period (tentative) | 1 June 2026 to 15 July 2026 |
| Exam shifts | 10:00 am–1:00 pm & 2:00 pm–5:00 pm |
| Expected hall ticket release | Last week of May 2026 |


IGNOU and multiple guides advise students **not to wait till the last day**, because centres fill on a first-come-first-served basis and the portal often slows down near the deadline.

#### **Eligibility to Fill the June 2026 Exam Form**
In simple terms, you can fill the June 2026 exam form if:
* You have a **valid registration** for the course(s) you want to appear in (registration or re‑registration still within programme validity).
* You have completed the **minimum duration** required for those courses/programme as per the programme guide (for example, first year completed before giving second-year courses).
* You have **submitted assignments** for those courses within the assignment deadline announced by IGNOU/regional centre (many portals mention 31 March or 30 April 2026 as tentative cut‑offs, but you must follow your official notice).
* You can also include **pending/backlog courses** from earlier semesters, provided your programme validity has not expired.

IGNOU’s general instruction is that an exam form is required **every June and every December** – it is never automatic or carried over.

#### **Official Website and Correct Portal**
For filling the June 2026 TEE exam form, always use official channels only:
* Start from **ignou.ac.in** → look under “Alerts/Announcements” or “Student Support” for “Online submission of examination form for June 2026 TEE”.
* The actual form is hosted on the **IGNOU Exam Portal: exam.ignou.ac.in**, which handles online TEE forms for ODL and Online programmes.
* Some guides mention IGNOU Samarth links, but those ultimately redirect to the official exam portal for the payment and form submission.
* Avoid Google-search shortcuts that might land on fake form pages; always reach the form through IGNOU’s main site or your regional centre website.

#### **Exam Fee and Late Fee for June TEE 2026**

**Normal exam fee (per course):**
* **₹200 per theory course (per subject)** is the standard exam fee.
* **₹300 per practical/lab course** is commonly mentioned, and some sources note that for certain project/practical‑heavy components it can go up to **₹500**.
* The exact rate appears on the live portal when you select that course.
* The system automatically calculates your **total fee** based on the number and type of courses selected.
* Payment modes are fully online: **debit/credit card, net banking, UPI**, etc., via a payment gateway linked from the exam portal.

**Late fee structure:**
* **No late fee** up to **10 April 2026** – only normal per‑course fee.
* From **11 April 2026 to 24 April 2026**, you can still submit the form but you must pay a **flat ₹1,100 late fee + normal exam fee per course**.
* After **24 April 2026**, the portal is expected to close; do not assume any extension unless IGNOU releases a fresh notification.

#### **June 2026 TEE Exam Schedule and Mode**

**Tentative date sheet:**
* Exams are scheduled **from 1 June 2026 to 15 July 2026**.
* The date sheet is available as a PDF on **ignou.ac.in**; you can search by programme and course codes to find your exact dates.
* Some well-known courses (like BSHF‑101, FST‑01, some library science and commerce papers) are in **objective/OMR format**, while others remain **descriptive (long‑answer) exams**.

**Shifts and exam mode:**
* There are **two shifts each day**:
    * **Morning:** 10:00 am – 1:00 pm
    * **Evening:** 2:00 pm – 5:00 pm
* The June 2026 TEE is being held in a **hybrid format**:
    * Largely **offline pen‑and‑paper** for ODL and many online programmes.
    * **Computer‑Based Test (CBT)** or OMR mode for certain online or purely objective courses.

#### **Step-by-Step Form Fill-up Process**
1.  **Open the official exam form link:** Go to **ignou.ac.in** and click “Online submission of examination form for June 2026 TEE”, or open **exam.ignou.ac.in** directly while the window is active.
2.  **Read the general instructions:** Review the instructions page carefully – it covers eligibility, fee rules, late fee, assignment requirement, cancellation/no‑refund policies, etc.
3.  **Tick the confirmation** and click on **“Proceed to fill the online examination form”**.
4.  **Enter your basic details:** Enrolment number (usually 10 digits), Programme code (e.g., MBA, BAG, BCOMG, PGDPM, etc.), and Exam centre region.
5.  **Choose your exam centre:** Use the “Click here to check examination centres” link to see available centres. Select an exam centre code; remember, **centre allotment is on First Come, First Served (FCFS) basis**.
6.  **Select your courses (theory and practical):** Tick all course codes for which you are eligible and want to appear in June 2026 – including backlogs if within programme validity.
7.  **Verify details and fee calculation:** Check that your name, programme code, exam centre, and course codes are correct. The system will display total exam fee (e.g., ₹200 per theory, ₹300+ for practical).
8.  **Pay the exam fee online:** Choose one of the available options – credit/debit card, net banking, UPI – and complete the payment via the secure payment gateway.
9.  **Download the acknowledgement/confirmation:** After successful payment, an acknowledgement slip/receipt (with transaction details and control number) is generated – **download, save, and if possible, print it**.
10. **Check form/payment status later if needed:** If money is debited but the status shows “Pending”, wait **48–72 hours** and then checking the “Exam form status” link instead of paying again immediately.

#### **Assignments, Projects and Practicals: How they link with TEE form**
* Assignments and TEE are closely linked: for each course, you typically must submit **assignments** by the official deadline (often **31 March or 30 April 2026** for June TEE 2026).
* For **project and practical courses**, IGNOU and regional centres separately announce submission schedules, viva dates, and practical exam arrangements.
* Filling the exam form without submitting assignment may lead to your **result being withheld**, even if you write the TEE paper.

---

#### **Hall Ticket, Exam Centre Finalisation, and Results**
* **Hall Ticket / Admit Card:** Expected around the **last week of May 2026**, about 7–10 days before the start of the exam. You will download it from **ignou.ac.in** using your enrolment number and programme details.
* **Centre Allotment:** Even if you chose a preferred centre while filling the form, the **final centre** is what appears on the hall ticket – IGNOU may change it if your chosen centre is full. Centre allocation is influenced by seat availability and FCFS timing of your application.
* **Result Mapping:** Only courses for which the **exam form was successfully submitted and fee credited** are counted in the June 2026 TEE evaluation for that session.

---

#### **Practical Tips and Common Mistakes to Avoid**
* **Do not wait till 10 April or 24 April:** Server load, payment failures and filled centres are more common near the deadline.
* **Check programme validity before paying:** If your programme validity is over, the form might still take the fee in rare cases, but you will not be allowed to appear.
* **Match course codes carefully:** Select only those course codes that are actually registered in your current/previous sessions for which minimum duration is over.
* **Don’t ignore assignments:** Many students fill the exam form but skip assignments, leading to withheld results.
* **Avoid double payment:** If one transaction is stuck, wait and track status; if you end up paying twice, the refund process takes time.
* **Use only official links:** Access **exam.ignou.ac.in** via **ignou.ac.in** or your regional centre.
* **On exam day, carry printouts and ID:** Take a clear printout of your hall ticket plus your IGNOU ID card or other accepted photo ID to the centre.
