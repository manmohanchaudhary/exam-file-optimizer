# **BPSC AEDO Exam 2026 Cancelled: New Exam Schedule Soon – Full Details & Preparation Strategy**

The Bihar Public Service Commission (BPSC) has officially announced that the **BPSC Assistant Education Development Officer (AEDO) exam 2026 has been cancelled**. This significant decision follows reports of widespread malpractice and sophisticated cheating attempts detected during the examination window. With over 1.1 million aspirants affected, the commission is now working toward a revised schedule to ensure a fair and transparent selection process.

In this comprehensive guide, we break down the reasons behind the cancellation, the current status of the re-exam, and how candidates should pivot their preparation strategy.

## **Why was the BPSC AEDO Exam 2026 Cancelled?**

The cancellation was not triggered by a question paper leak, but rather by **organized malpractice attempts**. The commission prioritized the "sanctity and credibility" of the recruitment process after discovering several irregularities.

### **1. High-Tech Cheating Scandals**

During the examination held between April 14 and April 21, district administrations detected organized attempts to facilitate cheating through **Bluetooth devices** and other electronic tools. These "anti-social elements" were caught red-handed at several centers, leading to the registration of 12 separate FIRs in districts such as Munger, Nalanda, and Samastipur.

### **2. The Biometric Agency Failure**

A major controversy erupted involving **M/s Sai Educare Private Limited**, the Jaipur-based agency responsible for biometric attendance and OMR barcode scanning. Investigations revealed that the agency was deputing personnel who were not on the BPSC's randomized approval list.

Crucially, it was discovered that Sai Educare had already been **blacklisted by the National Testing Agency (NTA)** in October 2025 for irregularities during a Gujarat High Court recruitment exam. Consequently, BPSC has blacklisted the agency and initiated the forfeiture of its bank guarantee.

### **3. Impact on APSWMO Exam**

The scandal was not limited to the AEDO post. The **Assistant Public Health and Waste Management Officer (APSWMO)** exam, held on April 23, 2026, was also cancelled after a biometric staff member and a candidate were arrested at AN College, Patna, with solved answer keys.

## **Actions Taken by BPSC**

The commission has taken a "zero-tolerance" stance toward the malpractice:

*   **Mass Debarment:** 32 candidates have been banned for life from appearing in any future BPSC examinations.
*   **Criminal Proceedings:** A total of 39 individuals, including the 32 candidates and external facilitators, have been arrested as part of ongoing investigations by the Economic Offences Unit (EOU).
*   **Transparency Measures:** BPSC clarified that while no paper leak was found, the attempts to affect the "sanctity of the exam" were serious enough to warrant a total restart.

## **BPSC AEDO Re-Exam Date & Schedule**

While the official **New Exam Schedule** has not been released, experts and sources suggest the re-exam will likely take place within **1 to 2 months** of the cancellation notice.

### **What's Next for Candidates?**

*   **New Admit Cards:** Candidates will not use their previous hall tickets. Fresh admit cards will be issued for the re-examination.
*   **Stricter Security:** The re-exam is expected to feature enhanced security, potentially including signal jammers, AI-based CCTV monitoring, and more rigorous frisking protocols.

## **Syllabus & Exam Pattern Refresh**

Since the exam is being reconducted, candidates should use this time to master the three objective papers. Each paper carries 100 marks and lasts 2 hours, with a **1/3rd negative marking** penalty for wrong answers.

### **Table: BPSC AEDO Exam Structure**

| Paper | Subject | Marks | Nature |

| **Paper 1** | General Language (30 English / 70 Hindi) | 100 | Qualifying (Min 30%) |
| **Paper 2** | General Studies | 100 | Merit-based |

### **Key Topics to Revise**

*   **General Studies:** Focus on Indian History (with Bihar's contribution), Geography of India & Bihar, Indian Polity, and National/International Current Events.
*   **General Aptitude:** Number System, Ratio & Proportion, Profit & Loss, Time & Work, Data Interpretation, and Logical Reasoning.
*   **Language:** Basic Grammar (Sandhi, Samas), Synonyms, Antonyms, and Sentence Correction in both English and Hindi.

## **Expert Preparation Tips for the "Final Stretch"**

Mentors like Dr. Akhilesh Kumar (former DSP) and Shasikant Sir recommend the following strategy during this delay:

1.  **Don't Start New Topics:** Use the extra time to perfect what you have already studied. Revision is more beneficial than new material at this stage.
2.  **Focus on Data:** Memorize key statistics, years of historical events, and mathematical formulas.
3.  **Analyze the Cancelled Papers:** The questions asked in the April shifts are the best indicator of the current "exam trend." Use them as practice sets.
4.  **Mental Calm:** The uncertainty of cancellation can be stressful. Maintain a disciplined routine to avoid burnout.
