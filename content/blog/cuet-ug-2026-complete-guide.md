The Common University Entrance Test (CUET UG 2026) has become the backbone of undergraduate admissions in India. Conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA), it provides a single, standardized platform for students seeking admission to Central, State, Private, and Deemed universities.

With major reforms introduced in 2026, CUET is now more structured, competitive, and transparent than ever before.

## 🧾 What is CUET UG 2026?

CUET UG is a national-level entrance exam that replaces multiple university entrance tests, simplifying the admission process for millions of students.

**Key Benefits:**
* **One exam** for 275+ universities
* **Equal opportunity** for all boards
* **Transparent** merit-based selection
* **Reduced** application burden

---

## 🔥 Major Changes in CUET UG 2026

The 2026 cycle brings significant reforms to streamline the testing process:

1. **Fully Computer-Based Test (CBT):** The exam is now conducted only online, eliminating offline mode completely.
2. **No Optional Questions:** 50 questions per subject, and all are compulsory.
3. **Subject Reduction:** The number of subjects has been optimized from 63 down to 37.
4. **Maximum 5 Subjects Rule:** Students can now choose a maximum of 5 subjects to focus their preparation.

## 🧠 Marking Scheme

Understanding the marking scheme is vital for managing negative marks.

* ✅ **Correct Answer:** +5 marks
* ❌ **Wrong Answer:** -1 mark
* ⏭️ **Unattempted:** 0

**Maximum Marks Potential:**
* **Per subject:** 250
* **5 subjects total:** 1250

## 📚 Subjects in CUET UG 2026

* **Section I: Languages:** English, Hindi, Tamil, Bengali, Marathi, Urdu, etc.
* **Section II: Domain Subjects:** Physics, Chemistry, Maths, Economics, Business Studies, etc.
* **Section III: General Test:** GK & Current Affairs, Logical Reasoning, and Quantitative Aptitude.

## ⚖️ Normalization Process (Very Important)

CUET uses percentile-based normalization to ensure fairness across different shifts and difficulty levels.

<div class="custom-step-block" data-number="1" data-title="Percentile Calculation">
Percentile is calculated for each candidate within their specific shift.
</div>

<div class="custom-step-block" data-number="2" data-title="Data Merging">
Candidate data is merged across all shifts for the same subject.
</div>

<div class="custom-step-block" data-number="3" data-title="Score Adjustment">
Scores are adjusted to account for variations in paper difficulty.
</div>

<div class="custom-step-block" data-number="4" data-title="Final Normalized Score">
A final normalized score is generated, which universities use for the merit list.
</div>

:::note
Universities use **normalized scores**, not raw marks, for final admissions.
:::
